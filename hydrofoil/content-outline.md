# Hydrofoil Content Outline

## 1. System Overview

Explain the hydrofoil as one coupled machine:

- Front wing generates most of the lift
- Tail provides stability and often a balancing force
- Mast transfers both hydrodynamic and rider-induced moments
- Rider stance changes the required tail and front-wing effort
- Board and tracks are structural members, not passive packaging

Interactive idea:

- Side-view schematic with draggable rider stance
- Show how center of mass moves relative to mast and center of lift

## 2. Lift, Drag, and Angle of Attack

Explain:

- Lift and drag generation in water
- Why lift rises with angle of attack until stall
- Why high lift is not automatically high efficiency
- Why "more lift" can still feel worse if it comes with more drag or worse balance

Interactive idea:

- Plot lift and drag curves for front wing and tail
- Slider for foil family: pump / surf / HA / race-like

## 3. Trim and Stance

Focus:

- Difference between standing in balance versus using the tail to compensate
- Rider center of mass versus system center of lift
- Why moving backward can reduce unnecessary tail effort and drag

Interactive idea:

- Sweep stance forward/back and show front-wing load, tail load, and total drag

## 4. Tail, Shim, and Stability

Focus:

- Tail as stabilizer and balancing surface
- Tail size, aspect ratio, and incidence tradeoffs
- Shim as incidence adjustment, not magic
- Stable and forgiving versus low-drag and exacting

Interactive idea:

- Compare larger forgiving tail against smaller faster tail
- Toggle "same balance point" versus "same stance" views

## 5. Pumping Mechanics

Focus:

- Why efficient pumping feels like jumping up off a firm platform
- Why front-foot-heavy pumping often wastes energy by pitching the board down
- Timing and projection: upward impulse, forward projection, recovery
- Why some foils tolerate bad technique more than others

Interactive idea:

- Animate one pump cycle with force vectors and energy flow
- Compare well-trimmed versus nose-heavy setups

## 6. Speed Regimes and Modes

Focus:

- Low-speed "turn mode" versus faster "efficiency mode"
- Why riders sometimes step forward when very slow
- Why bigger foils feel firm over a wider speed window
- Why HA foils can feel great at speed and fragile when slow

Interactive idea:

- Speed slider with stance and tail-load overlays

## 7. Surface Effects

Focus:

- Breach and loss of lift near the surface
- Ventilation paths down the mast
- Why ride height matters
- Difference between aerodynamic-style intuition and free-surface reality

Interactive idea:

- Ride-height slider showing lift degradation and ventilation risk

## 8. Structural Loads

Focus:

- Mast as lever arm into the tracks
- Front-box compression and rear-box tension under pump loads
- Why repeated cycles matter even without catastrophic failure
- Flex versus outright failure

Interactive idea:

- Free-body diagram with rider weight and pump impulse
- Show bolt loads and board sandwich stress zones

## 9. Scenario Library

Presets to make the article practical:

- Beginner standing too far forward
- Big pump foil with too much tolerance masking bad trim
- Mid-aspect surf foil in the sweet spot
- HA foil that feels amazing only when speed is maintained
- Powered wing stance versus unpowered glide stance
- Foil Drive transition from motor stance to pump stance

## 10. Validation and Honesty

The article should clearly label:

- What is simulated
- What is simplified
- What is inferred from rider reports
- What is only a heuristic
