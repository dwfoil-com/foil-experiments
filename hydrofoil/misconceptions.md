# Hydrofoil Misconceptions and Questions

This file translates rider/forum language into claims the project should explain or test.

## Trim and Stance

### Claim

Moving the feet farther back can make a setup feel more balanced, faster, easier to pump, easier to control, and able to fly slower.

### Physics question

Is the rider reducing the amount of stabilizing tail force required to balance the system, which lowers total drag and makes the foil feel "firm" under load?

### What to show

- Stance shift changes required tail lift
- Tail lift changes total induced drag
- The "sweet spot" is foil- and speed-dependent, not universal

## Pumping: "Jump Up" Versus "Push Down"

### Claim

Most beginners pump from too far forward, so their effort mostly pushes the nose down instead of projecting the system forward and upward.

### Physics question

How does stance location change the fraction of rider effort that goes into pitch-down moment versus useful foil loading and rebound?

### What to show

- Same pump input, different stance
- Compare nose-heavy and balanced setups
- Show why a well-trimmed foil can feel like a springboard

## Low End Means Two Different Things

### Claim

Riders often confuse:

- Minimum sustainable speed
- Recoverability / pumpability / tolerance when speed drops

### Physics question

Can a high-efficiency foil have a lower true stall speed in expert hands while still being less forgiving and less recoverable for average riders?

### What to show

- Speed floor versus handling margin as separate plots
- Big forgiving tail versus small fast tail

## Bigger Tail Helps, But Not For Free

### Claim

A larger tail can improve stability and error tolerance, even if it adds drag and lowers theoretical efficiency.

### Physics question

When is the added stability worth more than the drag penalty?

### What to show

- Larger tail widens the usable stance envelope
- Smaller tail shrinks drag but narrows the acceptable trim window

## "Firm" Versus "Mushy"

### Claim

Some foils feel like concrete when loaded, while others feel soft, vague, or easy to push through.

### Physics question

Is this mostly a combination of speed, foil family, stance, and proximity to the stable operating window?

### What to show

- "Firmness" as a rider-facing description of local load response
- Same foil at different speeds
- Different foil families at the same rider weight

## Step Back to Pump, Step Forward When Slow?

### Claim

Riders often move rearward to pump or glide efficiently, but may move forward again at very low speed or in specific transitions.

### Physics question

What stance minimizes drag at a given speed, and how does that differ from the stance that maximizes maneuver authority or recovery margin?

### What to show

- Efficiency mode and turn/recovery mode are not always the same position
- The optimal stance can migrate with speed

## Shim as Optimization Versus Compensation

### Claim

Some shim changes fix the setup. Others merely compensate for poor stance or foil placement.

### Physics question

How much of a setup change can be replaced by moving the rider, mast, or both?

### What to show

- Separate geometry changes from rider-input changes
- Show when shim reduces rider workload and when it hides bad trim
